Community District Distribution of Waste Reduction

As it was not feasible to display similar graphs and regression lines for all 59 community districts, we represent the slopes of the regression lines for per-capita refuse production in each community district over time as histograms (see above). We generated histograms showing the distribution of trash output of four years. The community level decrease in refuse production over time is evidenced as the distribution of refuse production shifts left between 1992 and 2008 in the histograms above. Approximately 17% of community districts decreased their refuse production at least 50% faster than the mean rate of decrease. These findings suggest the need for further studies examining whether the variation in rates of refuse production decrease between community districts is due to random variation or due to sociopolitical, environmental, or other pressures.
